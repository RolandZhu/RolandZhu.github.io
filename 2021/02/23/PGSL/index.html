Posted on 2021-02-23

Learn to Grasp with Less Supervision: A Data-Efficient Posterior Grasp Sampling Loss

Xinghao Zhu, Yefan Zhou, Yongxiang Fan, Lingfeng Sun, and Masayoshi Tomizuka

Welcome to this page. This page supplements our RAL2021 paper submission, in which we present a data-efficient posterior grasp sampling loss

Robotic grasping for a diverse set of objects is an essential component for any robot manipulation tasks. One promising approach is to learn deep grasping models from training datasets of object images and success grasp labels. Approaches in this category require millions of data to train deep models. However, empirical grasping datasets typically consist of sparsely-labeled samples (i.e., scarce successful grasp labels in each image). This paper proposes a Posterior Grasp Sampling Loss (PGSL) to tackle the data sparsity issue. The proposed method regards successful grasp labels are sampled from a ground-truth grasp distribution and aims to recover such dense map. PGSL is utilized for training a fully convolutional network that evaluates thousands of grasp candidates simultaneously. Training results suggest that models based on PGSL can learn to grasp with datasets compose of 2 labels per image, considering 8$\times$ more data-efficient than current state-of-the-art techniques. Meanwhile, physical robot experiments demonstrate an equivalent performance in detecting robust grasps at a 91.8% grasp success rate on household objects.
